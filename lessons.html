General comments regarding music instruction

Many music instructors base their knowledge from prior instructors and as such have not necessarily studied significant issues in historical musics. I have observed this in beginner instructors to conservatory and PhD level instructors. Traditional word of mouth music instruction has the limitation that if a student desires to know the evidence for the answer to a question, many the instructor may struggle to provide a suitable answer. The point is not condemn, but to question how the student should gain playing confidence if they do not comprehend the music itself. A good question for testing an instructors background knowledge is by asking What tempo should baroque piece be played at? There exists many historical documents that can be cited and multiple answers consistent with academics and musicologists.
No instructor holds all the answers at their finger-tips, but many seemingly glaring questions are left unanswered. As such, Alex advocates for evidence-based instruction and that instructors learn continously. It can be difficult to translate words to muscle movements. As such Alex's approach to instruction includes communicating in a thoughtful manner that helps students learn quicker.
